- **niche:** data
- **mood:** premium-luxe
- **style:** dark, photographic, 3d
- **palette:** bg `#0B1020` · ink `#F4F6FB` · accent `#3A6FD8` — Earth's blue atmospheric glow in the hero photo, button outlines/links, the AI Chat send button and accent icons in the embedded product UI; restrained — color comes mostly from the photographic backdrop rather than flat brand fills
- **type:** display *Geometric grotesque sans (Helvetica Now / Neue Haas Grotesk-class, ultra-tight tracking)* · body *Neutral humanist sans (Inter-class)* — Heavyweight, condensed-feeling, NASA-poster confidence; near-zero letter-spacing so the headline reads as a solid wall of type
- **sections:** hero › feature-ai › logos › feature-navigate › feature-insights › feature-byo-database › feature-control › feature-security › cta › footer
- **signature:** A full-bleed photoreal Earth-from-orbit + satellite hero where the headline sits in the dark space half and the product literally feels like it's 'in orbit' — the planet's terminator (day/night line) doubles as the contrast gradient that makes white type pop on the dark side and dark type readable on the lit side.
- **imagery:** Cinematic space photography (real Earth curvature, satellite, starfield) for the hero, transitioning down into crisp product-screenshot UI — the actual database table + AI Chat panel — floating on the dark gradient. The photo is the brand; the product shot is the proof. No abstract 3D blobs.
- **copy:** Plain-spoken outcome promise over jargon — 'We made working with data easy' — confident, first-person-plural, with subhead naming the audience (engineers, researchers, analysts) and the payoff (safety and security guaranteed).

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Let a single photoreal hero image do the color work: keep brand fills near-monochrome (dark navy + white type) and source your accent — here a blue planetary glow — from the photograph itself, so the page feels premium without a loud palette.
- Use a real environmental gradient (the planet's day/night terminator) instead of a CSS gradient to carry contrast: white type on the night side, product UI emerging from the lit side. The 'gradient' has narrative meaning.
- Oversize the headline to a tracked-tight type wall that spans the column edge-to-edge ('We made working with data easy' at ~hero-width), then immediately ground the abstraction with a literal product screenshot lower in the same scroll.
- Name security as a feature cluster of small concrete h3 chips (Two-Factor Auth, HIPAA, SOC 2 Type 2, SSH Tunneling, Data Encryption) — a grid of compliance terms reads as 'enterprise-ready' faster than a paragraph for a data/dev audience.
