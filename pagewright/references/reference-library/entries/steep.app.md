# Steep — https://steep.app

![steep.app](../thumbs/steep.app.jpg)

- **niche:** analytics
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** gradient, editorial, minimal
- **palette:** bg `#F3EDEA` · ink `#2A2A2E` · accent `#E9A38B` — soft peach-to-lilac aurora gradient washing the whole hero canvas; warm coral tints on the floating metric cards (registrations, activation) and avatar chips
- **type:** display *High-contrast transitional serif (Times/Georgia-like, with a true italic for emphasis words)* · body *Neutral grotesque sans-serif (Inter/Helvetica-like)* — Editorial and literary up top, calm and utilitarian below — a magazine masthead bolted onto a data tool
- **sections:** hero › feature-intro › feature-engagement › testimonial › feature-metrics › feature-analysis › cta › footer
- **signature:** A serif-italic headline floating over a warm pastel aurora gradient — the exact opposite of the dark, monospace, neon-grid look every other AI-analytics tool defaults to. It dresses a BI platform like a literary editorial.
- **imagery:** No photography or illustration. Real product UI fragments — region tables, line charts, KPI cards, an 'Ask anything' chat input, avatar chips — float as semi-transparent cards bleeding off the canvas edges, scattered asymmetrically around the centered text rather than boxed in a hero mockup.
- **copy:** Confident, plain-spoken value prop with a wink — real hero: "AI analytics for faster insights and zero chaos" (with 'zero chaos' set in italic serif for punch).

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Set the hero in a high-contrast serif and italicize the one emotional phrase ('zero chaos') to make a data product feel human and editorial.
- Replace the single hero screenshot with scattered, edge-bleeding UI cards at varying opacities — it implies a rich product without a heavy mockup.
- Use a warm peach-to-lilac aurora gradient on a near-white canvas to reject the dark-mode/neon cliche of AI tooling while still reading 'modern'.
- Drop a one-line founder/customer quote ('I have never seen BI adoption like this in my career') as its own section to make the engagement claim feel earned, not asserted.
