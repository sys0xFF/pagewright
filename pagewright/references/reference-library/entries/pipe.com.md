# Pipe — https://pipe.com

![pipe.com](../thumbs/pipe.com.jpg)

- **niche:** fintech
- **mood:** technical-dark
- **style:** dark, photographic, editorial-minimal, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#0A0A0A` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#FF4D1C` — First two words of the hero headline ("Pipe is"), the "Demo" nav CTA, and the small flame-mark logo — used as a sparse ignition point, never as a fill
- **type:** display *SFMono-Regular (monospaced system font used at large display sizes)* · body *SFMono-Regular (same mono carried into body copy)* — Engineer's terminal voice — monospaced everywhere gives the whole page a code-editor / infra feeling, signalling 'we are the financial OS, not a consumer bank'
- **sections:** hero › logos › feature-capital › feature-tools › feature-nocode › feature-speed › products › cta › footer
- **signature:** Setting the entire page in a monospaced font (SFMono) at display sizes — fintech almost always reaches for a warm humanist sans to feel trustworthy and approachable; Pipe instead leans into terminal/code typography to position itself as developer infrastructure (the 'financial OS'), and pairs it with a single hot-orange ignition accent on a true-black canvas.
- **imagery:** Full-bleed, unretouched 35mm-style documentary photography — a real person on the phone at a cluttered desk, slight film grain, naturalistic color (teal wall, magenta bottle). Deliberately un-corporate and warm to counterbalance the cold mono/black UI. Photo is clipped into a hard rectangular block occupying the right half, no rounded corners or device frames.
- **copy:** Infrastructure-positioning, B2B2B voice that names the category outright — hero: "Pipe is the embedded financial OS powering businesses on your platform" with subhead "Smart embedded tools that keep your customers' cash flowing so they never need to go anywhere else."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use a monospaced display face to instantly read as 'infrastructure/dev platform' instead of consumer fintech — the typeface alone does the positioning.
- Highlight only the first 2-3 words of the headline in the accent color so the eye lands on the subject ('Pipe is') before the claim — a cheap, high-impact emphasis trick.
- Pair a cold true-black + mono UI with warm, grainy documentary photography of a real human to add humanity without diluting the technical brand.
- Reserve a single hot accent (orange) for exactly three touchpoints — logo, highlighted word, primary CTA — so it functions as ignition, not decoration.
