# Oatly — https://www.oatly.com

![oatly.com](../thumbs/oatly.com.jpg)

- **niche:** food
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** editorial, hand-lettered, zine, off-beat
- **palette:** bg `#F2F0E6` · ink `#1A2436` · accent `#A9CBE0` — A chalky tile-blue that only appears inside the photo (the bathroom wall) and as one small tag chip ("OTHER"); the brand resists a marketing color and lets ink-navy do almost all the work.
- **type:** display *hand-drawn brush/marker lettering, knockout-on-black panels (à la a custom face like "Oatly Display" / Tungsten roughened)* · body *condensed grotesque all-caps, slightly irregular (think a quirky League Gothic / GT America Condensed)* — wry, lo-fi, talks-to-you-like-a-friend.
- **sections:** hero › manifesto-strip › products-grid › sustainability-report › faq › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero swaps the expected glossy carton beauty-shot for a deadpan documentary photo of a row of urinals in a tiled public bathroom, captioned with hand-painted scroll lettering that reads "PEE FOR THE PLANET" and the wall stencil "GÖDSEL." Self-aware, almost anti-design: the layout is a scrappy modular grid of black-and-tan cards (a "F.A.Q-ING AMAZING" panel, a "THE FUTURE OF TASTE REPORT" card) that looks more like a paste-up zine spread than a DTC homepage.
- **imagery:** Real photography, but intentionally un-styled and odd (a fluorescent-lit restroom, not a kitchen), layered with hand-lettered signage and scanned-marker headlines on flat black blocks. No 3D, no gloss; the charm is the homemade, photocopied texture.
- **copy:** Manifesto in plain talk, set as a left-aligned typewriter-ish block: "WE EXIST TO MAKE IT EASIER FOR PEOPLE TO LIVE HEALTHIER LIVES WITHOUT RECKLESSLY TAXING THE PLANET'S RESOURCES IN THE PROCESS." with a tiny "READ MORE →" pill. Card eyebrows pun relentlessly — "F.A.Q-ING AMAZING" over "FREQUENTLY ASKED QUEST…", and "THE FUTURE OF TASTE REPORT" tagged "NEWS".

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Replace the obvious product beauty-shot with a deadpan, almost ugly real-world photo and let a one-line pun do the selling — surprise beats polish.
- Build the page as a paste-up grid of black/tan cards with hand-lettered headlines so it reads like a zine, not a template.
- Keep the palette to warm paper + ink-navy and refuse a loud brand color; let typography and copy carry all the personality.
- Lean on puns and a first-person manifesto voice ("WE EXIST TO…") to make a commodity (oat milk) feel like a point of view.
