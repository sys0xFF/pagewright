# Trustditto — https://trustditto.com

![trustditto.com](../thumbs/trustditto.com.jpg)

- **niche:** CSR / ESG compliance SaaS (sustainability reporting copilot)
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, colorful, illustrated
- **palette:** bg `#ECEDE3` · ink `#1A1438` · accent `#3DBA3D` — progress bars inside the product UI card (compliance % meters), plus a green Trustpilot star; secondary brand splashes in yellow/lime/green/magenta arc behind the hero mockup
- **type:** display *High-contrast serif (Fraunces / Recoleta-style) for the oversized hero H1* · body *Geometric sans-serif (Poppins / Gilroy-style) for nav, subhead and UI* — Editorial-meets-friendly: a literary serif headline softens compliance dread, while the rounded sans keeps the product feeling approachable and modern rather than corporate-legal
- **sections:** nav › hero › logos › feature-speed › feature-platform › feature-knowledge-base › feature-questionnaire-automation › feature-insights › feature-partners › trust-tech-plus-human › testimonials › resources › cta › footer
- **signature:** The compliance dashboard is dragged half off the right edge of the viewport, bleeding behind a rainbow arc (yellow to lime to green) and a magenta blob — turning a dry ESG audit tool into a warm, almost playful poster. Compliance software almost universally goes navy-corporate-serious; this leans pastel-sage and editorial instead.
- **imagery:** Realistic in-app product screenshot (Frameworks grid with live green completion meters) shown as a floating rounded card, cropped at the page edge for momentum. Sits over organic, overlapping colored arcs/blobs (eco rainbow + magenta) that read as brand energy. Customer logos are mono-gray wordmarks under a single subhead, each tagged 'CASE STUDY >' so social proof doubles as navigation.
- **copy:** Empathetic, validation-first voice that flips compliance from chore to credit — hero: "Get the CSR recognition you deserve" with subhead "You're already doing the work. Ditto helps you prove it."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Lead with the user's hidden win, not the feature: 'you're already doing the work, we help you prove it' reframes a compliance burden as earned recognition.
- Use a literary high-contrast serif at massive scale over a sage/off-white bg to make a regulated, dry category feel premium and human instead of navy-corporate.
- Encode the product's value visually — live green progress bars on a framework grid (EcoVadis 94%, ISO 88%) show 'compliance %' as the hero proof, no abstract illustration needed.
- Turn the logo wall into navigation: append 'CASE STUDY >' under each customer mark so trust signals also become clickable proof paths.
