# Krepling — https://krepling.com

![krepling.com](../thumbs/krepling.com.jpg)

- **niche:** ecommerce / commerce platform (no-code store builder)
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, 3d, photographic
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#1A1A1A` · accent `#7C5CFC` — Get-started button outline, the purple Gusto/chat integration tile floating in the hero collage, and small CTA highlights
- **type:** display *Geometric grotesque sans (rounded-terminal, single-story a — Circular/Gilroy family)* · body *Same geometric sans at lighter weight* — Soft, friendly, confident — rounded geometric letterforms keep a powerful claim feeling approachable rather than corporate
- **sections:** hero › feature-build-no-code › feature-workflows › feature-storefront › feature-dashboard › feature-integrate › how-it-works › feature-grid › faq › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero rejects the standard ecommerce dashboard-screenshot. Instead, oversized soft-pink cylindrical 3D forms drift through the frame with real integration logos (Slack, Mailchimp, Gusto) physically embedded into them like cards slotted into sculpture — turning the integration list into a tactile, gravity-defying still life.
- **imagery:** Rendered 3D abstraction: pale-pink matte cylinders and a glass lens floating in negative space, lit softly with gradient blush tones. Recognizable brand logos are mounted on small colored tiles that intersect the sculptural shapes, blending photoreal product-render lighting with editorial collage composition.
- **copy:** Bold absolute claim paired with a plain-spoken promise — hero: "The world's most powerful commerce platform." with sub-hero "Build your store using clicks instead of code." and the reframe "Your online store should be a sales channel, not an engineering challenge."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Embed real partner/integration logos as physical objects inside 3D hero art instead of a flat logo strip — proof and aesthetic in one move.
- Anchor a grandiose headline ('world's most powerful') with a humble, concrete benefit line ('clicks instead of code') so confidence reads as helpful, not arrogant.
- Use a soft blush-on-white palette with one saturated purple accent so the page feels premium and calm while still flagging CTAs.
- Frame the value prop as a reframe of the user's pain — 'should be a sales channel, not an engineering challenge' — rather than a feature list.
