**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Shoot the hero as a low-angle, full-bleed street photo of the product in real use — sell the experience of wearing it, not a packshot on white.
- Let the headline whisper: thin white type tucked in a corner, sized to defer to the photograph rather than fight it.
- Use transparent outline pills for dual CTAs ("SHOP MEN" / "SHOP WOMEN") so the image stays the loudest element on the page.
- Hide your brand color inside the photograph (the navy tread text) instead of painting it onto the UI, so the chrome stays neutral and the product stays the hero.
