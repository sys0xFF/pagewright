- **niche:** fintech
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, photographic, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#F2F0ED` · ink `#1A1A1A` · accent `#F25C18` — primary CTA button, the 'See Brex in action' play link, the physical card edge accent, and a thin wiring/trace line across the hero photo
- **type:** display *Grotesque/neo-grotesque sans (Brex custom 'Brex Sans' style, à la Söhne/Aktiv Grotesk)* · body *Same humanist-grotesque sans, lighter weight, generous line-height* — Confident and engineered — heavy display weight with tight tracking reads like industrial product design, not playful fintech; lowercase, period-terminated headline gives it a calm, declarative authority
- **sections:** topbar-announcement › nav › hero › logos › feature-cards › how-it-works › feature-operations › feature-rewards › resources-insights › footer
- **signature:** The hero leads with a tactile, oversized product photograph of the physical black card resting on a soft pink-beige surface with a real phone showing the live app — shot like a luxury-goods still life with a faint orange circuit trace line drawn over it, instead of the flat dashboard-UI screenshot every fintech defaults to. Hardware-as-hero, not software-as-hero.
- **imagery:** Photoreal product still-life: matte-black metal Brex card and a real iPhone running the Wallet UI, staged on warm neutral seamless paper with soft studio shadows. A single thin orange vector line traces across the composition like a circuit/flight path, bridging physical object and digital UI. Premium, tactile, editorial-catalog treatment — minimal props, maximum surface texture.
- **copy:** Declarative, lowercase-confident product-design voice — short noun-led promises ending in a period. Hero: "Finance built for speed and control." with subhead "Modern cards, banking, expenses, accounting, and more — in 120+ countries."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Lead with the physical object as a luxury still-life (real card + real phone on warm seamless paper) instead of a flat product screenshot — makes a software company feel tactile and premium
- Use a warm off-white/greige (#F2F0ED) as the base instead of clinical pure white; it reads expensive and pairs with one hot orange accent used sparingly
- Terminate the big headline with a period and keep it lowercase-calm — declarative authority beats hype-y title case in fintech
- Draw a single thin accent-colored vector trace across the hero photo to visually wire the hardware to the on-screen UI — a cheap, repeatable motif that signals 'connected system'
