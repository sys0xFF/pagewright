# Posthog — https://posthog.com

![posthog.com](../thumbs/posthog.com.jpg)

- **niche:** analytics
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** illustrated, colorful, photographic
- **palette:** bg `#EEEAE2` · ink `#1D1F27` · accent `#F1A82C` — primary CTA buttons (Get started - free), the bottom toolbar Get started button, active tab underline, and small inline highlights; the warm tan desktop background is itself a secondary brand color
- **type:** display *Open Runde (rounded geometric sans for the H1/headings); a serif (Charter / Computer Modern) used for the operating-system-window body copy* · body *IBM Plex Sans Variable for UI chrome, with Source Code Pro for code and Comic Sans MS / Fairytale as deliberate joke fonts* — playful, soft-cornered, anti-corporate; rounded display sans signals friendliness while the retro-OS serif and gag fonts add irreverent personality
- **sections:** hero › feature-product-usage › logos › feature-data-stack › pricing › feature-ai › problem › blog › cta › footer
- **signature:** The entire page is staged as a faux desktop operating system: a tan wallpaper with skeuomorphic file/app icons down both rails (home.mdx, Pricing, Docs, Trash, customers.mdx) framing a draggable .mdx document window with a working text-editor toolbar. The marketing site literally cosplays as the product.
- **imagery:** Hand-drawn flat illustration as the dominant language - a bespectacled hedgehog mascot lounging at a desk, skeuomorphic OS app icons, and quirky spot art - mixed with one piece of meme photography (a real cutout person waving by the cookie banner). No product screenshots in the hero; the chrome itself is the product visual. Treatment is warm, sketchy, low-saturation earth tones with the orange accent popping against tan.
- **copy:** Plain-spoken, confident, slightly cheeky founder voice that names the pain before the product - hero H1 reads "The new way to build products" with subcopy "PostHog is the only platform that acts like a co-pilot for you (and your AI agents) to do it all - autonomously."; section labels lean into the joke ("Bedtime reading", "Shameless CTA", a "Legally-required cookie banner" with an inside gag).

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Stage the whole page as your product's interface: wrap marketing copy in a real, skeuomorphic app window (toolbar, title bar, draggable chrome) so visitors feel the product before they click anything.
- Use desktop-OS app icons as your nav/sidebar - turn boring menu links (Pricing, Docs, Changelog, Trash) into labeled illustrated icons down the page rails for instant warmth and navigability.
- Name sections with jokes instead of category labels ('Bedtime reading' for the blog, 'Shameless CTA' for the closer) to telegraph a non-corporate brand voice in the heading hierarchy itself.
- Deploy deliberately 'wrong' fonts as a personality device - mixing a rounded sans, an old-school serif, and even Comic Sans signals confidence and irreverence rather than sloppiness.
