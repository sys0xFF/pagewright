# Overflow — https://overflow.io

![overflow.io](../thumbs/overflow.io.jpg)

- **niche:** design
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** gradient, minimal, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#6A5CF0` · ink `#1A1A2E` · accent `#2EA8FF` — right-side cyan glow in the hero gradient, in-app connector UI chips, and link/CTA highlights
- **type:** display *Geometric grotesque sans (Sofia Pro / Gilroy-style)* · body *Same humanist-geometric sans, regular weight* — Friendly-confident; rounded geometric terminals keep it warm rather than corporate, heavy display weight gives editorial swagger
- **sections:** hero › feature-three-ways › feature-save-time › feature-story › feature-superpowers › testimonials › feature-wow-effect › how-it-works › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero is a full-bleed diagonal violet-to-cyan gradient with white type floating dead-center, then the actual product UI (a real diagramming canvas with a browser chrome) slides up from the bottom edge and overlaps the gradient — the marketing surface literally hands off to the working app instead of showing a flat screenshot in a card.
- **imagery:** Authentic product UI as the hero artifact — a macOS-windowed canvas of the diagramming tool with real connector toolbars, zoom controls, and a sample user-flow board peeking up from below the fold. No stock photography, no abstract 3D; the software itself is the imagery, framed in skeuomorphic browser/window chrome for trust.
- **copy:** Opinionated craft-outcome taglines; hero: "User flows done right" with narrative subhook "One design story. Three ways to tell it."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Let the hero gradient bleed edge-to-edge and float centered white type over it, then break the frame by overlapping the live product UI rising from the bottom — depth without a containing card.
- Use a directional two-hue gradient (cool violet sweeping into bright cyan) so the background itself reads as motion/light rather than a flat brand fill.
- Structure the whole page as a narrative arc of H2 hooks ('Tell your design story like never before', 'Discover your new superpowers') instead of generic feature labels — copy carries the personality, not decoration.
- Keep CTAs as solid near-black pills against the bright gradient for maximum contrast, pairing a bold 'Download' with a low-key '- free trial' modifier in lighter weight.
