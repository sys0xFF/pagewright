# Lattice — https://lattice.com

![lattice.com](../thumbs/lattice.com.jpg)

- **niche:** hr-people-management (HRIS / performance + AI)
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** gradient, clean, colorful
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#0A2E1E` · accent `#1F6B4A` — primary CTA button (with a rainbow gradient border), 'Platform overview' pill, link underlines, and the multi-hue gradient card framing the hero product shot
- **type:** display *Geometric humanist sans (Lattice's custom/Aktiv-Grotesk-like), tight-tracked, heavy weight* · body *Same humanist sans at regular weight, generous line-height* — Confident and modern but soft-cornered — big bold headlines feel assertive, yet the rounded letterforms and warm green ink keep it friendly rather than corporate-cold
- **sections:** announcement-bar › hero › logos › feature-platform-overview › feature-ai-agent › feature-habits › testimonials › feature-integrations › feature-modules-grid › pricing-cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero product screenshot sits inside a candy-rainbow gradient cushion (pink-to-mint-to-cyan) — an HR/enterprise category that almost universally defaults to safe single-color blue trust signals instead leans into a soft, joyful multi-hue gradient, making the software itself look approachable and human.
- **imagery:** Real product UI screenshots (review forms, weekly updates, employee avatars) layered on pastel gradient backdrops, plus a rounded floating tooltip ('Because the most effective teams don't wait for annual...') overlaid on the screenshot to narrate a benefit. Partner logos rendered in flat muted gray for an even, calm logo wall.
- **copy:** Outcome-led and inclusive — frames AI as a partner to people, not a replacement. Hero: 'People + AI: Succeeding Together' with subhead 'Join 5,000+ forward-thinking teams using Lattice's HR and AI tools to manage people and performance — all on one trusted platform.'

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Wrap your product screenshot in an oversized soft-gradient 'cushion' card instead of a flat drop-shadow — it injects warmth and color into otherwise clinical enterprise UI
- Put a rainbow/multi-hue gradient on the BORDER of the primary CTA so the button reads as the single brightest element on a near-white page
- Use a floating in-screenshot tooltip to editorialize the product image — it doubles as a micro-headline that sells the benefit, not just shows the feature
- Pair very heavy, tight-tracked display headlines with deep-green ink (not black) on white — keeps the page bold yet softer and more human than pure-black enterprise type
