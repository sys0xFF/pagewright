- **niche:** legal

- **style:** dark, photographic, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#0E0B08` · ink `#F2EFEA` · accent `#B5651D` — Not a saturated UI color — the 'accent' is the warm amber/cognac glow baked into the photography (whiskey, leather, lamplit library wood). - **signature:** The hero is a cinematic, dimly-lit photograph of a distinguished silver-bearded senior partner deliberating over paper in a leather-and-mahogany library — a real human in a real boardroom, not a product UI. An AI company chose to lead with the *craft of lawyering* rather than a dashboard, and let the serif headline float in the dark negative space on the left where the photo falls to black.
- **imagery:** Cinematic photography, color-graded dark and warm (amber key light against deep shadow), with a subtle glass/reflection overlay layering multiple figures around a conference table. Treated like a film still or a prestige-brand campaign, not a SaaS hero. Product screenshots are deferred entirely below the fold; the top of the page is pure mood.
- **copy:** Aspirational two-word serif aphorism over plain-spoken trust copy — hero reads 'Practice Made Perfect', subhead: 'Today's top law firms and in-house legal teams trust Harvey to elevate their craft and navigate complexity.' Voice = confident, understated, peer-to-elite-professional.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Lead with the customer's craft, not your UI: for a premium B2B tool, a cinematic photo of the practitioner at work out-sells a dashboard screenshot — defer all product shots below the fold.
- Build the accent INTO the photograph: instead of a saturated button color, color-grade the hero warm (amber/cognac against near-black) so the brand has a temperature without a single colored UI element.
- Anchor the headline in the dark side of the photo: shoot/crop so one half falls to black, then set a high-contrast Didone serif in that negative space — no scrim box needed.
- Pair an editorial old-money serif headline with a neutral grotesque body to signal 'established institution' rather than 'tech startup' — the type contrast itself does the positioning work.
- Run the client logo bar as a quiet greyscale strip immediately under the hero (named law firms + Merck/Comcast) so social proof lands before any feature claim.
