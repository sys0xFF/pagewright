**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Match the photo backdrop color to the page background so the subject sits *in* the page rather than on top of it — one continuous warm canvas, no boxed hero image.
- Use a deep desaturated green ink for serif headlines on a warm beige bg to feel like calm editorial wellness, not cold clinical SaaS.
- Reserve the only saturated accent (burnt amber) for a single CTA pill, and let the subject's wardrobe color quietly rhyme with it.
- Float a slim cool-toned announcement bar above the warm hero to carry a timely trust message ("insurance nationwide") without disrupting the main composition.
- Anchor a credibility strip of comma/dot-separated proof points ("50,000 Members Served", "Board-Certified Clinicians") right under the fold instead of a logo wall.
