- **signature:** The fold is built around a gamified "scratch-to-win" modal — a literal brushed-gold lottery ticket you drag to reveal a prize — layered directly over the editorial hero photo. Instead of a polished beauty close-up, the hero model wears the magenta eye masks unretouched, tattoos and friendship bracelets fully visible, slouched on a couch with chin on fists. It deliberately swaps aspirational gloss for an unfiltered, real-girl-in-her-room energy, then gamifies the discount instead of just slapping a banner on it.
- **imagery:** Photographic, warm natural light, shot like a candid portrait rather than a studio campaign — visible film grain, soft shadows, the magenta under-eye masks as the product proof point worn ON the face. No 3D, no illustration; the visual argument is "this is a real person using it."
- **copy:** Casual and game-show playful. Modal headline reads "Try your luck" with the subhead "Scratch below to see what you win", and the foil panel itself says "SCRATCH HERE" — turning the email-capture/discount moment into a little reward instead of a hard ask.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Gamify the discount: render the promo as a real-feeling scratch-off foil ticket ("SCRATCH HERE") so claiming a code feels like winning, not opting in.
- Let the product be worn in the hero — magenta eye-patches ON the model's face — so the "before" state is the visual, no separate diagram needed.
- Cast and shoot for realness: tattoos, bracelets, a slouched candid pose and visible grain read as authentic and out-perform glossy studio campaign shots for Gen-Z beauty.
- Keep the metallic accent confined to the one element that needs to feel valuable (the ticket), and leave the rest of the canvas neutral cream so it pops.
