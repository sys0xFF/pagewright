- **niche:** productivity
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** dark, colorful, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#2B1B36` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#E579D6` — italicized hero keywords (teams, data), primary CTA pill, logo wordmark, top utility bar, nav active states
- **type:** display *Geometric rounded sans (GT Walsheim / Toggl custom rounded grotesque) with a quirky italic cut for emphasis words* · body *Clean humanist sans, generous line-height* — Friendly, rounded, confident-but-approachable; the slanted handwritten-feel italics inject personality into an otherwise tidy geometric system
- **sections:** hero › feature-adoption › feature-productivity › feature-enterprise › how-it-works › feature-roi-calculator › cta › footer
- **signature:** Deep aubergine/plum dark mode for a time-tracking tool — the category lives in clinical white dashboards and corporate blue, so a saturated purple canvas with candy-pink accents reads as bold and human instead of "enterprise utility."
- **imagery:** Sparse, illustrative and brand-forward rather than product-screenshot-heavy in the hero. Flat playful spot illustrations (cookie mascot in the consent banner), rounded pill geometry everywhere, video-play affordance baked into the secondary CTA. Color and type carry the visual weight, not photography.
- **copy:** Plain-spoken, slightly cheeky benefit framing with selected words italicized for rhythm; hero: "Where teams and time tracking data meet."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use selective italic word-swaps inside an otherwise upright headline to add voice and direct the eye — color + slant only on the 2 words that matter (teams, data).
- Pair a fully pill-shaped button system (filled candy CTA + outlined ghost with inline play icon) to make a dual-CTA hero feel cohesive and tactile.
- Pick an unexpected saturated dark base (plum/aubergine) for a utility category to break out of the white-dashboard sea while still feeling premium.
- Let the cookie/consent banner carry brand personality with a mascot illustration instead of treating compliance UI as an afterthought.
