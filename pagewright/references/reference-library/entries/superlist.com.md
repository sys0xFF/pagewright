# Superlist — https://superlist.com

![superlist.com](../thumbs/superlist.com.jpg)

- **niche:** productivity
- **mood:** technical-dark
- **style:** dark, gradient, bold, cinematic
- **palette:** bg `#1A1326` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#FF3D24` — second headline line, primary CTA pills (Sign up / Sign up for free), logo mark, and small status dots in the product UI
- **type:** display *Geometric grotesque sans (heavy/black weight, very tight tracking) — reads like a custom or Aeonik/Sharp-Grotesk-style face* · body *Clean humanist sans (regular weight) for subhead and UI labels* — Loud and confident up top, calm and utilitarian in the product — the type does the shouting so the UI can stay quiet
- **sections:** hero › feature-everyday-superpowers › feature-meeting-notes-ai › feature-recurring-tasks › feature-heatmaps-previews › problem-stop-juggling-apps › cta-balance › footer
- **signature:** The hero headline is split into two colors stacked as a single oversized statement — line one in white, line two ("Finally in one app.") in vivid red — turning the value prop into a typographic punchline rather than decorating it with an illustration. Massive black-weight type with negative-tight tracking fills the full width, which breaks the polite, friendly-pastel convention of the to-do/productivity niche.
- **imagery:** A single large, realistic product screenshot floating in the dark hero — a real Superlist workspace (Production Planning board with action items, AI meeting transcript, and an embedded photographic thumbnail of a video shoot). The UI is shown at near-native fidelity with a subtle perspective/glow, sitting on a deep aubergine-to-magenta radial gradient that makes the white app card pop. Imagery language = show the actual product, no abstract 3D or mascots.
- **copy:** Plainspoken-but-punchy: a flat factual list ("Tasks, notes, and plans.") resolved by a defiant red kicker — hero reads "Tasks, notes, and plans. Finally in one app."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Two-tone headline: keep line one neutral/white and drop the emotional payoff onto a second line in your single accent color — the color does the persuading.
- Use one saturated red as the entire accent system (headline, CTA, logo, micro-status dots) against a moody gradient so every interactive element reads as 'the same action'.
- Let an oversized black-weight grotesk at full bleed carry the hero, then go deliberately quiet and utilitarian inside the product screenshot for contrast.
- Anchor a dark, dramatic hero with a single high-fidelity real product shot (including a photographic element inside the UI) instead of abstract art — it proves the app is real without a separate demo section.
