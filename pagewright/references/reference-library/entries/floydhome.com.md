**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Stage the hero as a believable lived-in moment (rumpled bed, half-eaten cookies, glasses left out) rather than a sterile studio shot — imperfection signals "real home, real durability."
- Bury the product in context instead of hero-lighting it; let the room sell the lifestyle and let the headline name the actual SKU in a small eyebrow.
- Keep the brand accent color off the hero entirely — confine the loud cobalt to a utility announcement bar so the photography's warm naturals carry the whole mood.
- Pair a humble, anti-maximalist headline ("Everything you need, nothing you don't.") with a small outlined button to read as honest and editorial, not salesy.
