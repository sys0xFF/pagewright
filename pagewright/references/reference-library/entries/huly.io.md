# Huly — https://huly.io

![huly.io](../thumbs/huly.io.jpg)

- **niche:** productivity
- **mood:** technical-dark
- **style:** dark, gradient
- **palette:** bg `#0A0B14` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#5B6CFF` — The hero light beam and ambient glow (blue→violet); secondary warm-orange glow on the 'SEE IN ACTION' pill CTA and small status tags inside the product UI (Low/Devops chips, progress ring).
- **type:** display *Geometric grotesque sans (Clash Display / Cabinet Grotesk-like)* · body *Neutral humanist sans (Inter-like)* — Confident, modern, slightly editorial — heavy display weight paired with a calm, legible body
- **sections:** hero › feature-productivity › feature-collaboration › feature-github-sync › feature-metabrain › feature-knowledge › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero's central vertical light beam — a luminous blue/violet shaft shooting up out of the product screenshot as if the app is radiating energy, turning a dashboard into a near-religious horizon glow.
- **imagery:** Product-screenshot driven: a real Tracker/Kanban + Inbox UI floats up from the bottom edge, fused into a dramatic abstract-3d light beam — a vertical shaft of blue-violet light erupting from the app like a horizon flare. Cinematic, dark-room product photography mood.
- **copy:** Bold two-tone declaration: "Everything App for your teams" (second clause in lighter weight), then names the giants it replaces — "all-in-one replacement of Linear, Jira, Slack, and Notion." Confident, category-claiming voice.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Two-weight headline trick: set the first clause in heavy white and the second clause in a lighter/translucent weight ('Everything App' bold, 'for your teams' light) to add rhythm without a second font.
- Let the product UI bleed off the bottom of the hero instead of framing it in a tidy browser mock — anchoring the screenshot to the viewport edge makes it feel like a window into a live app.
- Use a single directional light source (here a vertical beam) emanating FROM the product to unify an otherwise flat dark page and direct the eye, instead of scattering decorative gradients.
- Pair a cool dominant accent (blue-violet) with one tiny warm contrast glow (orange on the CTA) so the call-to-action pops against an all-blue dark scheme.
