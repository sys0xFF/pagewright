# Public Goods — https://www.publicgoods.com

![publicgoods.com](../thumbs/publicgoods.com.jpg)

- **niche:** nature
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, photographic, editorial, product-forward
- **palette:** bg `#D7E3E0` · ink `#1A1A1A` · accent `#0E0E0E` — There's effectively no chromatic accent: the only "color" is the soft teal-blue gradient of the staged shelves and the warm tan of a natural sea sponge. Emphasis is carried by a flat black wordmark, black headline, and a solid black "Discover More" pill button.
- **type:** display *neo-grotesque sans, Helvetica Now / Aktiv Grotesk family, bold weight, tight* · body *same grotesque, regular, gray* — Plain, unfussy, retail-confident; the wordmark "PUBLIC GOODS" is widely letter-spaced caps, a quiet pharmacy-label voice.
- **sections:** hero › category-grid (bath-body / home / cleaning) › membership-pitch › sustainability-story › bestsellers › bundles › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero is built as two horizontal "glass shelves" with a faint teal underglow, and the entire product family is lined up on them like a real apothecary display — shampoo, conditioner, body wash, a candle, then below a toothbrush cup, deodorant, razor, comb, perfume, toothpaste. The cropped right edge (toothpaste tube half off-frame) signals "the line continues," turning a flat product wall into a browsable shelf you scan left-to-right.
- **imagery:** Photographic product still-life, studio-lit on cool pastel backdrops. Every bottle wears the same minimalist cream label with small caps type, so the range reads as one cohesive system; the lone natural sponge and wood comb inject organic texture against the otherwise clinical white-plastic uniformity.
- **copy:** Calm, declarative, almost zero marketing adjectives — headline "Simple starts here", subhead "Everyday essentials, thoughtfully simplified", top promo bar "Become a member to unlock exclusive discounts," CTA "Discover More."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Stage an entire product range on a literal "shelf" so the catalog itself becomes the hero — breadth communicates value better than one beauty shot.
- Crop the rightmost product half off-frame to imply the line keeps going and invite horizontal scanning.
- Unify a diverse range with one identical label system (cream stock, small-caps name) so visual consistency does the brand-trust work.
- Drop the accent color entirely: let a soft pastel set + pure black type + black CTA pill read as premium minimalism, not a discount circular.
