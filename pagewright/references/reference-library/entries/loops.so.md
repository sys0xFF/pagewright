- **niche:** dev-tools
- **mood:** editorial-minimal
- **style:** minimal, mono-type, clean-light
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#2B2A28` · accent `#E2502A` — Logo mark only (the orange spiral icon top-left); the entire rest of the page is grayscale ink-on-white, making the single warm accent function as a brand signature rather than a UI color
- **type:** display *A high-contrast transitional serif (Times/Georgia-like, e.g. a refined book serif) used for the oversized hero headline* · body *A neutral grotesque sans-serif (Inter/Helvetica-like) for sub-copy, nav, and buttons* — Literary meets engineered. The serif headline reads like a book title or magazine masthead while the sans body keeps it crisp and modern. The pairing signals taste and restraint over startup-template energy.
- **sections:** hero › feature-email-design › feature-developer-experience › feature-lifecycle-workflows › feature-growth › feature-marketing-product-sync › feature-integrations › testimonials › cta › footer
- **signature:** A massive book-serif headline ("One platform, every email") set centered on stark white in a category — email/dev-tools — that defaults to bold geometric sans and gradient hero art. Swapping the expected SaaS sans for an editorial serif and removing all hero imagery is the genre-breaking move.
- **imagery:** Near-zero decorative imagery above the fold. Pure typographic composition: text on white. The only graphic elements are the orange spiral logo mark and a row of grayscale customer wordmarks (Framer, Linear, Perplexity, Granola, Clerk, Reuters, Y Combinator). A small rotating "Customer since" proof card anchors the logo wall. Treatment is flat, high-whitespace, zero shadow or gradient.
- **copy:** Confident, plainspoken one-liner with an underlined-keyword subhead; hero reads "One platform, every email" / "Loops is the simplest way to send product, marketing, and transactional email, from waitlist to IPO."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use ONE warm accent for the logo only and run the entire page grayscale — scarcity makes the brand color feel premium instead of decorative.
- Pair an oversized editorial serif headline with a neutral sans body to break the SaaS-sans monoculture and signal taste.
- Inline-underline the three product nouns (product, marketing, transactional) inside the subhead so the value prop doubles as a navigable feature map.
- Anchor the logo wall with a rotating 'Customer since [date]' micro-card — it converts a static trust strip into living, specific proof.
