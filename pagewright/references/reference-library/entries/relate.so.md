# Relate — https://relate.so

![relate.so](../thumbs/relate.so.jpg)

- **niche:** crm
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, gradient
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#1A1A2E` · accent `#2D6BFF` — single highlighted word in the hero headline ('Sales'), product-logo glyph, the blue-to-white sky gradient panel behind the hero, and live pipeline status dots/dollar figures in the embedded app UI
- **type:** display *Inter (or near-identical geometric grotesque)* · body *Inter* — Calm, modern-utilitarian sans with tight tracking and medium weight; reads like product UI text rather than marketing display type — deliberately understated and trustworthy
- **sections:** hero › feature-product-ui › feature-prospect › feature-close › feature-retain › feature-recycle › feature-grid › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero dissolves directly into a giant, pixel-faithful screenshot of the actual product (a full sales pipeline with real-named competitor logos — Linear, Framer, Loops, Mintlify — as fake deals). The marketing page IS the app; there's almost no abstraction layer between pitch and product.
- **imagery:** High-fidelity product screenshots presented as the primary hero visual, not decoration. Kanban-style deal cards with brand favicons, dollar amounts, and status pills. A soft cyan-to-white sky gradient panel frames the hero text like an open horizon, then the crisp white app UI sits on top — atmosphere above, precision below.
- **copy:** Plainspoken value-prop headline with one word color-flagged; voice is confident and B2B-literal. Hero: "Modern Sales CRM for B2B companies."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Color-flag a SINGLE word in an otherwise all-ink headline (the blue 'Sales') instead of styling the whole line — cheap, high-contrast emphasis that points the eye exactly where you want it.
- Use a soft sky-gradient panel (cyan fading to white) as an atmospheric stage that the hard-edged white product UI emerges from — pairs 'aspiration' with 'precision' in one viewport.
- Populate product demo screenshots with REAL recognizable brand names as sample deals (Linear, Framer, Loops) — instant credibility and 'companies like you' signaling without a logo wall.
- Structure the feature sections as a verb-cycle (Prospect / Close / Retain / Recycle) so the headings themselves narrate the customer lifecycle the product manages.
