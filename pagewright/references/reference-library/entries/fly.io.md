- **imagery:** Editorial hand-drawn illustration: textured colored-pencil/gouache linework in a fairy-tale register, asymmetric and full-bleed, with the artwork wrapping the headline instead of sitting beside it. No product screenshots in the hero; the 'sandbox' concept is visualized as a literal animal-in-a-sandbox scene at lower right.
- **copy:** Confident two-beat imperative with a wink — short declarative claim plus a playful adverb. Hero headline: 'Build fast. Run any code fearlessly.' with 'fearlessly' in italic script; subhead: 'The platform for devs who just want to ship.'

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Set the hero word that carries the emotion (here 'fearlessly') in a contrasting italic script with a hand-drawn underline — one word becomes the brand voice and the only decoration the headline needs.
- Replace the dashboard screenshot with a single panoramic illustration that literally enacts your core metaphor (sandbox = a creature playing in a sandbox), so first-time visitors grasp the concept before reading a word.
- Pair a serious transitional serif headline against a clean sans body to signal 'credible infrastructure' while the illustration carries all the playfulness — gravitas and whimsy split across type vs. art.
- Keep the chrome quiet: pill nav on a near-white lilac wash, one saturated violet accent reserved only for CTAs and the hero underline, letting the riot of pastel illustration read as warm, not noisy.
