# MERIT — https://www.meritbeauty.com

![meritbeauty.com](../thumbs/meritbeauty.com.jpg)

- **niche:** beauty
- **mood:** editorial-minimal
- **style:** minimal, photographic, editorial, off-white
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#1A1A1A` · accent `#9AA7B2` — There is no marketing color at all; the only non-neutral is the slate-blue announcement bar at the very top, and even the "Shop Now ▸" CTA is plain black text + a tiny arrow, no button fill. Restraint IS the brand signal.
- **type:** display *high-contrast italic editorial serif (Didone, à la Canela / GT Sectra Display Italic)* · body *humanist grotesque (à la Söhne / Akkurat), tiny and gray* — Fashion-magazine masthead voice over near-anonymous sans copy.
- **sections:** hero › shop-by-category › bestsellers › find-my-shade-quiz › press-quotes › refill-sustainability › cta › footer
- **signature:** The headline "Spring | Summer 2026 Collection" is set in a large, slanted, high-contrast italic serif — stacked over four lines, ragged-right, with a literal `|` pipe baked into the wordmark as if it were a runway season title from a fashion house, not a beauty drop. Paired with a tightly-cropped, bare-skin, no-makeup-makeup portrait that fills a clean white rectangle floated in negative space, the whole fold reads like the cover of a print quarterly rather than an e-commerce hero.
- **imagery:** Single editorial photograph — an extreme close-up portrait, soft daylight, visible skin texture and freckles, neutral gray top, eyes to camera. No retouching gloss, no product bottle in frame, no 3D. The product is implied entirely through the model's "lit-from-within" skin.
- **copy:** Quiet luxury, no exclamation marks. Eyebrow "LIMITED EDITION", headline "Spring | Summer 2026 Collection", subhead "Our favorite lip and cheek shades, now in curated sets for an effortless, everyday look." CTA "Shop Now ▸". Top announcement bar: "The Sets Collection — get everything you need, for less. Shop Now."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Borrow the fashion-runway "Season Year Collection" framing for a product drop to instantly signal editorial taste over discount-driven commerce.
- Kill the button: make the primary CTA plain ink text + a hairline arrow so the fold reads like a magazine, not a storefront.
- Float a clean-edged photo rectangle in wide white space instead of a full-bleed background — the margin itself becomes the luxury cue.
- Pair a slanted high-contrast italic Didone headline with a tiny anonymous gray sans body to get tension between glamour and clinical neutrality.
