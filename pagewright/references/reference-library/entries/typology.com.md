# Typology — https://us.typology.com

![typology.com](../thumbs/typology.com.jpg)

- **niche:** beauty
- **mood:** editorial-minimal
- **style:** minimal, photographic, editorial, macro
- **palette:** bg `#F1EEEA` · ink `#1A1A1A` · accent `#E8A088` — The "accent" isn't a UI color at all; it's the peach-coral hue of the actual product serum blobbed across the right half of the hero. Everything chrome (nav, headline, button text) is plain near-black on warm off-white; the only saturation on the page comes from the product itself.
- **type:** display *grotesque sans, all-caps, loose-tracked — think Founders Grotesk / Söhne* · body *same family, regular weight, sentence case* — Quiet, clinical, lab-notebook restraint; type whispers so the product can speak.
- **sections:** hero › diagnostic-quiz › bestsellers › ingredient-transparency › routine-builder › reviews › sustainability › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero is one enormous macro photograph of the actual serum — a glossy peach droplet smeared across a pale surface, lit so you see every viscous highlight and the dry powdery edge where it dries down. It bleeds off the right and top edges, full-bleed and uncropped by any frame, while the text sits small and left-aligned in the negative space of bare background. The product texture itself IS the hero image; no model, no bottle, no styling — just the goo, photographed like a science sample.
- **imagery:** Macro product photography, clinical and tactile. Extreme close-up of the serum's physical texture on a neutral surface, soft natural lighting, real shadows and surface sheen. Reads more like a dermatology slide than a beauty ad — zero illustration, zero 3D, zero lifestyle.
- **copy:** Stripped-back, ingredient-first, almost SKU-like. Top promo bar: 'Your skin evolves; your routine should, too. Take your diagnostic.' Eyebrow-fused headline 'NEW — SKIN TEXTURE REFINER T30', subhead 'Smoother texture, more even skin tone. No pigments.' The product code (T30) is treated as part of the name — engineered, not romantic.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Make the product's raw texture the hero by shooting one full-bleed macro of the actual substance — no bottle, no model, no frame — and let its natural color be your only accent.
- Keep all chrome (nav, headline, CTA) flat near-black on a warm off-white so the single photographic hue carries 100% of the page's saturation.
- Fold the eyebrow into the headline as a dash-prefixed 'NEW —' and append a clinical product code (T30) so the name reads engineered rather than marketed.
- Use the top promo bar for a one-line skin philosophy plus a soft CTA ('Take your diagnostic') instead of a discount — sets a consultative, science-led tone before the hero even loads.
