![synthesis.com](../thumbs/synthesis.com.jpg)

- **niche:** education
- **mood:** bold-loud
- **style:** vivid, gradient, product-ui, friendly
- **palette:** bg `#1E2A8C`→`#27144E` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#F5B127` — A warm amber/gold that does double duty: it's the primary CTA pill ("Try it for free") AND the live game tokens inside the product screenshot, so the marketing color and the in-app color are deliberately the same. Body copy drops to a soft periwinkle (`#A9B4E8`) against the deep blue.
- **type:** display *geometric grotesque, heavy weight, tight set — think Aktiv Grotesk / Söhne Kräftig* · body *neutral humanist sans (Inter / system)* — Confident and parental-but-fun; the headline is set big and bold like a promise, not a textbook.
- **sections:** hero › how-it-works (adaptive tutor) › subjects-and-ages › parent-testimonials › pricing › cta › footer
- **signature:** The fold is split: a flat, oversized text promise on the left, and on the right a stacked, glowing "device" mock that layers a real talking-head video of a kid ("We are going to learn math like you've never learned it before.") directly on top of a live game-board screenshot of the actual product — multiplication grid, number pad, "11 × 7 = ?" prompt, "24 questions left in round". A Play-video pill bridges the two. It sells the emotional payoff (a happy, engaged child) and the literal product UI in one composed stack.
- **imagery:** Hybrid: candid child video-still (warm, real, not stock-polished) fused with a game-style product-UI screenshot rendered in dark amber-on-navy. Plus a tiny cluster of rounded family avatars beside the social-proof line. No illustration, no abstract 3D — just real kid + real app.
- **copy:** Warm, reassuring, parent-facing. Headline: "Your child's personal math tutor." Subhead: "Warm, patient, encouraging. The Tutor adapts to your child, so you never have to worry about them falling behind. Immediate results for less than $1/day." Social proof: "Join over 25,000 forward-thinking families. Designed for ages 5-11."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Unify the marketing accent with the in-product accent — the same gold on the CTA and on the game tokens makes the button feel like a piece of the product, not an ad.
- Stack two proof-types in one hero visual: an emotional video-still of a real user over a literal screenshot of the working UI, bridged by a Play button.
- Pre-empt the buyer's actual fear in the subhead ("never have to worry about them falling behind") and disarm price up front ("less than $1/day").
- Pin a precise number to the social proof ("25,000 forward-thinking families", "ages 5-11") with a small avatar cluster instead of vague "trusted by thousands".
