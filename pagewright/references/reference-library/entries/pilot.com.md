- **niche:** fintech
- **mood:** bold-loud
- **style:** bold, colorful, photographic
- **palette:** bg `#3B1E73` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#F5C518` — Mustard-yellow halftone circle haloing the photo subject; secondary violet (#8B5CF6) drives the primary CTA pill, monospaced eyebrow labels, and underlined text links.

- **sections:** hero › logos › problem › feature-guidance › how-it-works › cta-lead-magnet › pricing › footer
- **signature:** The conversational chat-bubble collage: faux CFO/founder text-message bubbles ("Bridget, your CFO → Your books are ready", "you're my hero!") pinned beside a die-cut human photo, turning a dashboard demo into an emotional dialogue instead of a feature shot.
- **imagery:** Cut-out studio photography (a real smiling CFO, masked from background) composited over flat color, framed by a mustard halftone-dotted circle, with floating product-UI chips (net-income chart card, chat bubbles "Your books are ready," "you're my hero!") collaged around her — product-screenshot + photography hybrid, scrapbook collage treatment.
- **copy:** Anxiety-as-headline, second-person interrogation. Real hero: "If you're buried in bookkeeping, who's leading the company?" — names the founder's pain, then answers it with a human CFO.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Lead with the customer's emotional question, not your product noun — a full-sentence interrogative H1 ('who's leading the company?') outperforms a feature headline in a high-anxiety category.
- Disarm a B2B product demo by collaging it as a text-message thread: pair real chat bubbles ('you're my hero!') with a die-cut human photo so the software feels like a relationship.
- Use a single warm accent (mustard halftone circle) as a spotlight against a saturated cool background (deep purple) so one human face becomes the only focal point on the screen.
- Wrap utilitarian eyebrow labels and CTAs in monospace caps to signal 'fintech rigor' while keeping the hero copy warm and human.
