- **niche:** design
- **mood:** technical-dark
- **style:** dark, minimal, cinematic
- **palette:** bg `#100A1E` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#5B4BFF` — top announcement-bar gradient, the primary 'Start Free Trial' pill, and the selected-asset highlight ring inside the product UI
- **type:** display *Tightly-set geometric grotesque sans (Frame.io custom, in the Neue Haas Grotesk / Aktiv Grotesk family)* · body *Same humanist grotesque at regular weight* — Confident, oversized, near-editorial — one typeface does all the work, set huge and tight on the hero and capped with a period for a declarative, full-stop tone
- **sections:** announcement-bar › nav › hero › logos › feature-deliver-faster › feature-remove-blockers › metric-2.9x-faster › feature-upload-organize › metric-2.7x-review › feature-review-accuracy › metric-31pct-churn › feature-share-present › showcase-reinventing-workflows › pricing › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero gives the right ~55% of the canvas to a pixel-real, fully-populated product UI — a dark video-asset browser packed with real film stills, filenames, collaborator names and dates — instead of an abstract gradient or hero illustration, and lets it bleed off the right edge so the interface itself becomes the imagery.
- **imagery:** Cinematic, moody film stills (a lit corridor, a bottle product shot, on-set portraits) rendered as thumbnails inside a realistic dark dashboard. Everything is desaturated to sit against the near-black canvas, so the only color comes from the footage itself — the page borrows the warmth of real creative work instead of decorative graphics.
- **copy:** Plain-spoken, benefit-first and declarative; the hero compresses the entire scope into one line — "One platform for all your creative work." — ending on a full stop that turns a feature claim into a manifesto.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Quantify the value prop literally in section headings — '2.9x faster workflows', '2.7x faster review', 'reduces churn by 31%' — alternating metric headers with feature headers so the page reads as proof rather than pitch.
- Punctuate the hero headline with a period; the full stop reframes a capability statement as a confident declaration and adds voice for free.
- Use the live product UI as the hero art and let it bleed off-frame, populated with believable on-brand sample data (film stills, real-sounding filenames, collaborators, file sizes) so it reads as an active session, not a static mock.
- Hold the palette to near-black + white + a single electric indigo, reserving the accent for the announcement bar, the one primary CTA, and the in-product selection ring so color always means 'act here'.
