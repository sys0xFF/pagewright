- **signature:** A scroll-synced split screen: the left rail holds a numbered vertical index (01 Morning Briefing -> 07 Daily Recap) acting as a table-of-contents, while the right panel is a single persistent product canvas that swaps its live UI mock as each index row activates. The feature list IS the navigation, and the demo never leaves the screen.
- **imagery:** Product-UI-as-hero rendered in glass: real-looking app chrome floating on a gradient-mesh backdrop. Frosted app-icon tray with Gmail/Calendar/Slack/Notion and red unread badges, a translucent status pill ("Dimension — Reviewing team chats..."), and a floating glass nav. No photography, no abstract 3D — the imagery is the product simulating an OS desktop. Treatment: heavy backdrop-blur, soft inner highlights, generous rounding, subtle drop shadows.
- **copy:** Confident anthropomorphic one-liner that sells an outcome, not features — hero reads "The AI coworker that never sleeps." (eyebrow: "Introducing Dimension"), backed by checklist-style benefit lines each led by a tiny mono icon.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Turn your feature list into the page's spine: number the capabilities (01-07) in a sticky left rail and let the active row drive one persistent product canvas on the right — saves you from 7 repetitive feature blocks.
- Go nearly monochrome (white ink on a gradient) and spend your ONLY saturated color on functional UI signals — here the red unread badges — so the accent reads as 'the product is alive' rather than decoration.
- Build a gradient mesh that travels emotionally: cool corporate blue at the top fading to warm peach/sand at the bottom, mirroring the 'overnight -> morning briefing' narrative of the product itself.
- Render the hero as a faux-OS: frosted app-icon dock with familiar third-party logos + a translucent status pill showing the AI mid-task ('Reviewing team chats...') — proves integration breadth and live agency in one glance, no copy needed.
