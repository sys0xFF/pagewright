# Kindsight — https://kindsight.io

![kindsight.io](../thumbs/kindsight.io.jpg)

- **niche:** ai (fundraising/nonprofit intelligence — donor analytics SaaS)
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** editorial, warm, photographic
- **palette:** bg `#3D3D2B` · ink `#F5F1E8` · accent `#C9E265` — the italic display word 'opportunities' in the headline, the hand-drawn underline flourish, and the orange 'Get a Demo' CTA button as a secondary accent
- **type:** display *A high-contrast transitional serif (Didone/Old-Style hybrid, ITC-Garamond-esque with very tall ascenders and a true italic cut)* · body *A neutral humanist grotesque sans (Inter / Aktiv Grotesk family)* — Literary and editorial up top, calm and utilitarian below — the serif gives nonprofit warmth and gravitas while the sans keeps the product story legible and modern
- **sections:** announcement-bar › hero › logos › feature-find-donors › feature-build-relationships › feature-grow-giving › products-overview › sectors-served › differentiator-values › community › service › support › cta › resources › footer
- **signature:** Setting a B2B analytics product against a deep olive/army-green canvas with an oversized serif headline where the payoff word ('opportunities') drops into a chartreuse italic underscored by a loose hand-drawn pen flourish — it reads like a fundraising appeal letter, not a data dashboard, deliberately rejecting the cold blue-gradient SaaS default.
- **imagery:** A collaged, slightly tilted cluster of overlapping product-UI cards (Major Gifts Dashboard, donor score chips, capacity donut charts, AI-generated email drafts) layered over a candid lifestyle photo of a smiling person at a laptop — fusing real human warmth with the analytical product surface so the data feels person-centered rather than clinical.
- **copy:** Empathetic, mission-first voice that positions software as a values partner — hero: "Fundraising intelligence that unlocks opportunities" with subhead "Forget stale spreadsheets and ancient tools."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use an unexpected earthy-green canvas + chartreuse accent instead of the default SaaS blue to instantly signal 'mission/nonprofit' warmth while still feeling premium.
- Mix a literary high-contrast serif for the hero with a neutral sans for the product body — let the typographic register shift carry the brand emotion.
- Color only the single payoff word in the headline (here italic 'opportunities') and underline it with a hand-drawn flourish to direct the eye and add human, hand-made texture.
- Collage tilted, overlapping real product-UI cards on top of a candid human photo so the analytics feel attached to a person and a mission, not a sterile dashboard.
