# Craft — https://craft.do

![craft.do](../thumbs/craft.do.jpg)

- **niche:** productivity
- **mood:** warm-playful
- **style:** illustrated, colorful
- **palette:** bg `#A9D4F0` · ink `#1C1C1E` · accent `#0A0A0A` — Black pill CTAs ('Try Craft Free') and the wordmark/nav; the colorful accents live inside the product cards (coral, mint, butter, lavender tabs) rather than the chrome
- **type:** display *A high-contrast Didone serif (Tiempos/Canela-style) for the H1 — thin hairlines, sharp bracketed serifs* · body *A clean geometric/grotesque sans (Inter-like) for nav, buttons, and UI labels* — Editorial-meets-friendly: literary serif headline gives warmth and craft, the sans keeps it modern and app-like; the chunky rounded CRAFT wordmark adds the playful note
- **sections:** hero › feature-versatility › how-it-works › feature-integrations › feature-planning › cta › footer
- **signature:** The whole hero is a layered cut-paper diorama — a sky-blue construction-paper sky with textured clouds, torn mountain ridges, and a notebook-page edge — that the real app window nests into, so the product looks like it lives inside a handmade scene rather than floating on a gradient.
- **imagery:** A collaged "paper landscape": cut-paper clouds, torn-paper mountains, and a yellow legal-pad edge built into a sky scene, with a realistic product-screenshot app window (note cards) tucked into the foreground like it sits on a desk in that world.
- **copy:** Benefit-led, plainspoken. Hero literally reads "Your space for notes, tasks, and big ideas" — warm and human, no jargon; section heads have personality ("Planning that doesn't feel like work", "Craft isn't just for one thing, it's for your things").

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Pair a high-contrast Didone serif headline with a neutral geometric sans body — the serif carries all the 'craft/warmth' so the rest of the UI can stay plain.
- Keep chrome monochrome (black pills, black wordmark on a single flat sky-blue field) and let ALL the color come from inside the product screenshot's colored note cards — the app supplies the 'colorful', not the page.
- Build the hero as a tactile paper-collage diorama (cut clouds, torn mountains, legal-pad edge) and seat the product window into it, instead of the default screenshot-on-gradient.
- Give every feature section a human, anti-corporate heading like 'Planning that doesn't feel like work' rather than a feature noun.
