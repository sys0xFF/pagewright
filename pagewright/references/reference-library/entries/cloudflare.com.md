- **signature:** The entire above-the-fold is flooded edge-to-edge in the brand's saturated orange with a faux-sunrise glow rising from the bottom — infra/dev pages almost universally go technical-dark or clean-white, so a full-bleed warm-orange hero with a sunrise gradient is a genuine convention-break that reads as a brand statement, not a SaaS template.
- **imagery:** Almost no literal imagery in the hero — the visual is pure color field plus a subtle dot/grid texture and a radial light bloom at the horizon. Brand mark (orange cloud) is the only icon. Imagery is replaced by typographic scale and atmospheric gradient; downstream sections introduce planet/"Region: Earth" framing.
- **copy:** Bold, plainspoken authority flexing scale as proof — hero reads: "Everything we learned from powering 20% of the Internet—yours by default"

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Flood the whole hero in one saturated brand color and let an oversized, tightly-tracked headline carry the page — no product screenshot needed above the fold.
- Add an atmospheric 'sunrise' radial glow rising from the bottom edge to give a flat color field depth and an optimistic horizon.
- Turn raw scale stats into headline copy ('20% of the Internet', '42% of the Fortune 500') so the brag IS the value prop, not a separate logo bar.
- Use poetic geographic framing like 'Region: Earth' as a section header to make commodity infra feel planetary and ambitious.
