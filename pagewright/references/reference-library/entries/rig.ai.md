# Rig — https://rig.ai

![rig.ai](../thumbs/rig.ai.jpg)

- **niche:** dev-tools
- **mood:** bold-loud
- **style:** brutalist, mono-type, colorful
- **palette:** bg `#FF3B21` · ink `#0D0D0D` · accent `#FF3B21` — The accent IS the hero background — a saturated red-orange flood fills the entire top fold; below the fold it inverts to near-black (#141414) with the same red used for tiny mono labels, ticket-notch button outlines, and the X-prefixed section tags like 'X THE PROBLEM'
- **type:** display *Chivo Mono (heavy/black weight, used at huge display sizes)* · body *Chivo Mono (regular weight for paragraphs and uppercase tracked-out labels)* — Single monospaced family doing everything — terminal-grade, engineered, anti-marketing; the black weight reads as a chunky grotesque while the small caps read as a code editor
- **sections:** hero › logos › problem › feature-everything-local › feature-work-offline › feature-purpose-beats-scale › feature-machine-unleashed › feature-built-for-control-freaks › cta-waitlist › faq › cta › footer
- **signature:** A fold that flips ideology with color: the hero is an aggressive all-red 'on-device' manifesto, then the page drops into a black surveillance-themed 'problem' fold ('You don't own your AI. And you're being watched.') — the red-to-black inversion is the narrative, not just a section break. Buttons are die-cut ticket shapes with a notched corner, reinforcing the hardware/terminal motif.
- **imagery:** Almost no photography or product screenshots in-fold — imagery is typographic and structural. A giant ghosted 'RIG' wordmark bleeds faintly behind the hero (tone-on-tone red), a horizontal marquee/ticker of uppercase mono claims ('100% OFFLINE', 'YOUR HARDWARE, YOUR RULES', 'NO TOKENS, NO LIMITS') separates folds, and feature blocks are numbered (001, 002) like spec sheets. Treatment is flat, hard-edged, zero gradients or rounded softness.
- **copy:** Confrontational privacy-manifesto voice that names an enemy ('big AI') — hero reads 'On-device AI coding. No cloud. No limits.' with sub 'A complete coding agent that executes entirely on your machine. No API calls. No usage caps.'; section heads are accusatory and short ('They train on your code.', 'They meter your ambition.', 'Built for control freaks').

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use ONE monospaced family across the entire page (Chivo Mono here) — black weight for billboard headlines, regular tracked-out caps for labels — so the type itself signals 'made by engineers' without any decorative fonts
- Make the color shift carry the story: flood the hero in a single hot accent, then hard-cut to near-black for the 'problem' fold so the inversion dramatizes the freedom-vs-surveillance pitch
- Die-cut your CTAs: notch one corner of each button into a ticket/badge shape and outline them in 1px accent stroke for a hardware-spec feel instead of a soft rounded pill
- Frame features as a numbered spec sheet (001 / 002...) with terminal-style tags ('X THE PROBLEM') to make a dev tool feel like documentation rather than a sales page
