- **niche:** legal-ai (contract review & drafting for legal teams)
- **mood:** technical-dark
- **style:** dark, editorial, photographic, cinematic
- **palette:** bg `#0E1116` · ink `#FFFFFF` · accent `#F0552B` — primary CTA buttons (Book a Demo), logo gem mark, and green check-bullet accents in the floating overlay card
- **type:** display *High-contrast transitional serif (Times/Georgia-class, likely a refined serif such as Tiempos or PP Editorial New)* · body *Humanist geometric sans-serif (Inter/Graphik-class) for nav, subheads and body* — Old-world legal authority (serif) deliberately collided with clean modern SaaS sans — gravitas meets velocity
- **sections:** hero › how-it-works › feature-intake › feature-first-pass › feature-search › feature-review › feature-drafting › feature-agent › feature-citations › feature-benchmarking › feature-security › stats › audience-split › testimonials › case-studies › compliance › cta › footer
- **signature:** A wide cinematic photo of an empty stadium as the hero image — legal-AI convention is abstract UI screenshots or sterile document mockups, but here a literal real-world venue grounds the contracts in commerce, with a floating glass card listing actual deal contracts ("120 contracts in place before the pitch opens") layered over the photo to tie product to a tangible scenario.
- **imagery:** Full-bleed, desaturated cinematic stock photography (an empty sports stadium) used as an emotive scene rather than a product shot; a translucent dark glass overlay card with green-check list items floats over the image to bridge real-world context and software output. Imagery is editorial and scene-setting, not screenshot-driven.
- **copy:** Confident, benefit-first with a velocity theme — hero serif headline "Contracts at the speed of commerce" over the plain-spoken subhead "Review contracts, draft with your standards, and search every deal you've signed."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Pair a high-contrast serif display with a clean sans UI to signal 'trusted institution + modern speed' — lets a regulated/legal product feel both credible and fast.
- Replace the obligatory product-UI hero screenshot with a real-world cinematic scene, then float one small glass data-card over it to translate abstract software into a concrete situation.
- Anchor trust inline next to the CTA rather than in a separate strip: 'Trusted by 4,500+ legal teams' + a G2 4.7 star rating sit right beside the buttons where the decision happens.
- Lead the section flow with a verb-driven workflow narrative (intake -> first pass -> put to work) so feature headings read as a process, not a feature dump.
