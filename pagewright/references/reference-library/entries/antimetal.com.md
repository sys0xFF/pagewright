# Antimetal — https://antimetal.com

![antimetal.com](../thumbs/antimetal.com.jpg)

- **niche:** devops
- **mood:** editorial-minimal
- **style:** minimal, mono-type
- **palette:** bg `#D8D6CD` · ink `#1A1A17` · accent `#23201C` — Reserved for the primary 'Book a demo' pill button and the active nav floating-pill chrome; nearly monochrome, so the accent is a near-black warm charcoal that pops as a solid filled CTA against the warm paper field. Black is also used for the constellation of dots in the hero graphic.
- **type:** display *A high-contrast transitional/old-style serif (Didone-leaning, in the vein of Tiempos / Freight Display / Lyon) — sharp brackets, pronounced thick-thin stroke modulation* · body *The same serif family at smaller size for the subhead/body, keeping the page single-typeface* — Literary, editorial, almost academic-paper — confident and quiet, the opposite of typical devops sans-serif tooling
- **sections:** hero › problem › feature-new-layer › how-it-works › feature-agents-platform › feature-agent-modules › blog-research-log › cta › footer
- **signature:** A living network-graph constellation occupying the entire right half of the hero — scattered black dots of varying sizes wired by faint thin lines, evoking a distributed production system as an organism rather than a UI screenshot. It reads the page's promise ('autonomous system for production') as a visual abstraction, not a dashboard mockup.
- **imagery:** Abstract-3d / generative network-graph as the hero motif: black nodes + hairline edges on warm paper, no product chrome, no gradients. Treatment is flat, ink-on-paper, almost scientific-diagram. The rest of the page (per headings) leans on conceptual diagrams of the Team→Agents→World Model→Production stack rather than literal app screenshots — keeping the editorial restraint throughout.
- **copy:** Declarative, almost manifesto-like product claims in plain confident sentences; hero headline reads 'Production that runs itself.' with subhead 'Antimetal is the autonomous system for production. Continuously understanding, operating, and improving your environment.' Voice is calm and authoritative ('Everyone else watches. We operate.').

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Set the whole page in a single high-contrast serif — using an editorial Didone for a devops/infra product immediately signals 'this is research-grade, not another dashboard SaaS' and differentiates in a sans-saturated category.
- Float the nav as discrete rounded pill-capsules (left links group, centered logo capsule, right CTA group) hovering over the paper rather than a full-width bar — gives a soft, app-like, almost OS-toolbar feel.
- Render your core concept as a generative node-graph instead of a screenshot: dots-and-hairlines on a warm neutral ground abstractly 'shows the product' while staying mysterious and ownable.
- Warm off-white paper (#D8D6CD) plus near-black ink, with the accent spent entirely on ONE filled pill CTA — extreme restraint makes the single dark button the only place the eye is pulled to click.
- Use a mixed-state secondary CTA: the 'Explore the research' button sits inside a dashed/corner-bracket frame (a blueprint marker) next to the solid primary — pairs a 'commit' action with an 'investigate' action visually.
