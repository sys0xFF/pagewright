- **signature:** The hero stages a real Gartner Magic Quadrant chart — a literal analyst scatter-plot — as the hero image instead of a product UI or abstract render. Most security pages bury third-party proof in a logo wall; CrowdStrike makes "we are the top-right dot" the entire visual argument of the fold.
- **imagery:** Cinematic dark red-to-black gradient stages with diagonal laser/light-streak textures cutting across the hero, evoking motion and threat-tracking. Imagery alternates between authoritative data artifacts (the Gartner quadrant screenshot framed like a document) and editorial threat-intel cards. Almost no stock photography — the visual language is data-as-hero plus atmospheric light.
- **copy:** Declarative, dominance-framed authority claims that lead with rank and rhetoric over features. Hero (carousel): "A Leader for the seventh consecutive time"; primary h1: "The Agentic Security Platform. Unified and built to secure the AI revolution."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Turn third-party validation into the hero itself: frame a real analyst chart or ranking as the fold's main image rather than hiding it in a trust strip lower down.
- Lead the headline with a count-streak claim ('seventh consecutive time') — quantified longevity reads as harder proof than an adjective like 'best'.
- Use a single saturated brand red against near-black as a directional tool: reserve it strictly for alerts, CTAs, and eyebrow labels so every red element commands action.
- Add diagonal light-streak gradients over a dark stage to imply velocity and surveillance — cheap atmosphere that makes a flat dark hero feel kinetic and on-theme for security.
