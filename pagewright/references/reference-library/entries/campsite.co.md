- **signature:** A near-empty white poster hero — the entire top viewport is just a huge two-line headline floating in whitespace with no graphic, no gradient, no chrome — and the actual product UI is pushed dramatically below the fold as one enormous high-fidelity app screenshot, with warm ember light leaking in from the bottom corners. It trades the standard SaaS 'hero-image-beside-copy' split for a magazine cover that withholds the product, then rewards the scroll.
- **imagery:** One oversized, pixel-perfect screenshot of the real product (three-pane app: sidebar nav, feed of posts, people-online rail) rendered crisply with a subtle macOS window chrome and rounded corners. Real human avatars and real-looking content (names, post titles, tags) make it feel lived-in rather than mocked. The only non-UI imagery is a soft orange ember/spark glow bleeding into the lower corners — warmth against an otherwise sterile white canvas.
- **copy:** Aspirational-contrast one-liner that reframes a category: hero reads "Teamwork, meet deep work" with subhead "The new standard for thoughtful team communication — replace noisy chats with focused, organized posts." Voice is calm, opinionated, anti-noise.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Lead with a 'X, meet Y' antithesis headline that pits two familiar concepts against each other — it positions the product as a new category in three words.
- Withhold the product: make the hero a near-blank poster and reward the scroll with one giant, lived-in app screenshot below the fold instead of the usual copy-beside-image split.
- Use a single hot accent (one orange) sparingly — logo, one pill, status dots inside the screenshot — and let a soft corner ember-glow inject warmth into an all-white page without adding a second color.
- Section headings written as full opinionated sentences ('Posts are the sweet spot between chat and docs') double as the product's argument, not just feature labels.
