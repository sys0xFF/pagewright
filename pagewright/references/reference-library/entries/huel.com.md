# Huel — https://huel.com

![huel.com](../thumbs/huel.com.jpg)

- **niche:** food
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** clean, photographic, editorial, grid
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#15110D` · accent `#C97B3C` — The warm terracotta/amber isn't a UI color; it lives almost entirely inside the photography (paper-bag backgrounds, the cocoa drink, the cereal bowl) so the page reads as a clean white frame around warm food imagery. A small lime-green `Give 25%, Get 25%` referral chip is the only true UI accent.
- **type:** display *near-grotesque sans (Aktiv Grotesk / Neue Haas Grotesk feel) mixed with a contrasting italic serif (Tiempos / Canela italic)* · body *humanist sans, regular gray* — Confident and editorial; the serif italic injects warmth into an otherwise utilitarian sans.
- **sections:** hero › goal-tiles (lose weight / more protein / eat healthy / on-the-go) › shop-your-way (bestsellers / powdered meals / bundles) › nutrition-science › reviews-ugc › sustainability › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero replaces a single hero image with a row of four equal goal-tiles — each a square photo card with a dark label chip and a circular `→` button (Lose weight on black, More protein over a pour of cocoa, Eat healthy over a steaming bowl, On-the-go over a Huel bar). It turns the fold into a self-segmenting menu: you click your goal, not a product. The mixed sans/italic-serif headline (`Food to *fuel* your goals`) is the brand's signature typographic move, repeated in `Shop *your* way` below.
- **imagery:** High-key studio product photography on warm paper-bag backdrops — real food staged with motion (cocoa mid-pour, steam rising off the bowl) rather than flat-lay packshots. Bottles and bars shot as hero objects with soft shadows. No 3D, no illustration; the warmth is entirely lighting and surface.
- **copy:** Plain, goal-first, benefit-driven. Headline `Food to fuel your goals` (the word `fuel` set in an italic serif), subhead `Complete nutrition that supports your body, your goals, and your life.` CTA pill `Shop All`; nav prompt `Which Huel is right for you?`; tile labels read as outcomes — `Lose weight`, `More protein`, `Eat healthy`, `On-the-go`.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Replace the single hero shot with a row of goal/outcome tiles so the visitor self-segments by intent in the first scroll.
- Keep the canvas pure white and let warm color live only inside the photography — the food becomes the palette.
- Mix one italic contrast serif into a grotesque headline on a single emphasized word (`fuel`, `your`) to add voice without a second typeface system everywhere.
- Stage product photos with implied motion (a pour, rising steam) instead of static packshots to make a clean grid feel appetizing and alive.
