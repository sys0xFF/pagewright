- **niche:** dev-tools

- **style:** minimal, mono-type, photographic

- **sections:** nav › announcement-banner › hero › feature-open-secure › how-it-works › feature-govern-agents › industries › feature-products › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero refuses the dev-tool dark-mode cliche: it's a stark all-white canvas with oversized near-black display type, and the only 'color' is a soft pink-to-lilac gradient bleeding out from behind the product screenshot — a quiet, almost editorial warmth under an otherwise austere engineering page.
- **imagery:** A single large, realistic product-UI screenshot in a dark macOS-style window (showing live agent workspaces — Running/Stop/Restart states, Claude Code task threads), floated on a pastel gradient backdrop. Photographic-real interface over color-wash, not abstract illustration or 3D.
- **copy:** Confident parallel-structure declaratives that frame a tension (speed vs control); hero reads: "Secure environments where devs and agents work in parallel."

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Use a soft pastel gradient (pink->lilac) glowing out from behind a dark product screenshot to inject warmth into an otherwise black-and-white engineering page
- Brand the logotype with a single geometric glyph swap (filled square replacing a letter) instead of a full custom mark — cheap, memorable, on-theme for infra tools
- Write H2s as paired antithetical phrases ('Open by design. Secure by default.' / 'Code at speed. Stay in control.') to dramatize the core product tension
- Show the real product running with live status states (Running, Stop in 4 hours) rather than a sanitized mockup — proof-of-life over polish
