# Cal — https://cal.com

![cal.com](../thumbs/cal.com.jpg)

- **niche:** productivity
- **mood:** clean-light
- **style:** minimal, bento
- **palette:** bg `#FFFFFF` · ink `#0F0F12` · accent `#1A1A1A` — Primary 'Sign up with Google' CTA fill and 'Get started' nav button are near-black; the only chromatic color comes from the calendar's selected-date chip (slate #3E4A5E) and the third-party review badge logos (green Trustpilot, red Capterra/G2 stars).
- **type:** display *Cal Sans (geometric grotesque; rounded terminals, single-story a, tight tracking, very heavy weight)* · body *Inter / neutral grotesque sans* — confident, friendly-geometric, oversized and tracked-tight — playful but enterprise-trustworthy
- **sections:** hero › logos › feature-ai-calls › how-it-works › feature-bento › testimonials › feature-grid › cta › footer
- **signature:** The hero pairs a giant left-aligned headline with a LIVE, interactive product widget on the right — an actual booking card (avatar, event title, 15m/30m/45m/1h duration pills, location, timezone) sitting next to a real month calendar with selectable dates. The product demos itself in the hero instead of showing a static screenshot.
- **imagery:** Product-UI as hero art: real, rendered booking components (not screenshots) floating on white inside a large rounded-corner container with a soft border. No photography, no 3D, no gradients — the interface chrome IS the imagery. Social proof rendered as authentic third-party badge logos (Trustpilot/Capterra/G2) rather than star glyphs alone.
- **copy:** Plain-spoken benefit headline, no jargon — hero reads "The better way to schedule your meetings" with a supporting line naming all three audiences (individuals, businesses, developers); voice is direct, inclusive, low-hype.

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Make the hero's right column a functional product fragment — duration pills (15m/30m/45m/1h) and a clickable calendar — so the value is demonstrated, not described.
- Stack a low-friction primary CTA ('Sign up with Google' with provider logo), a secondary 'Sign up with email' ghost button, and a tiny 'No credit card required' reassurance line directly beneath — a three-tier commitment ladder.
- Wrap the entire hero in one big rounded-corner card with a hairline border floating on near-white, then run a muted 'Trusted by fast-growing companies' logo strip just outside/below it as a quiet sub-band.
- Run a near-monochrome palette and let the ONLY color enter through borrowed trust badges (green Trustpilot, red review stars) — color signals credibility, not brand decoration.
