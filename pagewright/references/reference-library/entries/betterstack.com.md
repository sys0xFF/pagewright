- **signature:** A single product screenshot rendered in dramatic 3D perspective — tilted and receding into the dark, bleeding off the right edge so the dense log/telemetry data dissolves into shadow. It sells \"depth of data\" literally, and a carousel of dots cycles it through nine different product surfaces from one hero frame.
- **imagery:** Product-screenshot as hero, but treated as abstract-3d: the real dashboard UI is angled in z-space, edge-bled, and dimmed into a near-black gradient so individual rows read as texture rather than legible content. Customer logos sit in a low-contrast greyed-out monochrome strip below the fold.
- **copy:** Competitor-anchored value attack — name the incumbent and undercut it. Hero: \"The AI SRE observability stack\" with subhead \"30x cheaper than Datadog. Predictable pricing. Exceptional customer support.\"

**Takeaways (steal as ideas, don't copy):**
- Anchor the whole pitch on a named competitor's pain: a "30x cheaper than Datadog" subhead plus a migration-bribe microcopy line ("the rest of your contract is on us") does the qualifying that a generic value-prop can't.
- Use an email-capture input fused directly to the CTA button as the hero's primary action instead of a plain button — it shortens the funnel to one field on the dark hero.
- Turn ONE tilted, edge-bled product screenshot into the entire visual system: dim it into the background gradient so dense data reads as atmospheric texture, then put a dot-carousel under it to rotate through every product surface without adding sections.
- Build the page as a long stack of ~10 parallel feature blocks each labeled like a product SKU (Tracing, Log management, Error tracking), signaling platform breadth — depth-by-enumeration rather than a single hero feature.
