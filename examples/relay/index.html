The 3 AM problem

A webhook fired. Nobody caught it.

Your service is fine. The receiver had a 30-second blip. But that one dropped event becomes a missing payment, a desynced inventory count, a support ticket you can't reproduce. Hand-rolled retry loops don't scale — and they hide the failures you most need to see.

The hand-rolled way

  • — A try/catch with sleep(5) and a counter you forgot to cap
  • — Failed payloads vanish into log lines nobody greps
  • — No idempotency, so retries double-charge customers
  • — A receiver outage takes your whole worker queue down with it
  • — "Did it deliver?" is unanswerable after the fact
with relay

The delivery layer

  • + Exponential backoff with jitter, capped & configurable per endpoint

From send to delivered

One POST. We take it from there.

  1. 01

    Send us the event

    One authenticated POST /v2/events. We accept it in under 40ms and return a durable event ID. Your request is done.

  2. 02

    We route & transform

    Match the event to its subscriptions, run your transformation, sign the payload, and enqueue it on a durable per-endpoint queue.

  3. 03

    Deliver & retry until it sticks

    Attempt delivery with backoff + jitter. On failure we keep trying for up to 24 hours; anything still failing lands in the dead-letter queue, not the void.

  4. ✓

    You watch it happen

    Every attempt — request, response headers, body, timing — is in the inspector. Replay any event with one click or one API call.

Questions engineers actually ask

How is this different from just retrying in my own worker?

Your worker shares fate with your app — a deploy, a crash, or a receiver outage stalls the whole queue, and your retry state lives in memory you can't inspect. Relay is a durable, isolated layer: events survive your restarts, every attempt is recorded and replayable, and a slow receiver can't back-pressure your own request path.

How do you prevent duplicate deliveries?

Pass an idempotencyKey when you send an event. Relay dedupes on it for 24 hours, and we forward a stable relay-attempt header so your receiver can be idempotent too. Retries never re-trigger the same side effect.

What happens to events during a receiver outage?

They wait. Events sit durably in a per-endpoint queue and retry on a backoff schedule for up to 24 hours. Anything still failing after that lands in the dead-letter queue, where you can fix the receiver and re-dispatch in bulk — nothing is silently discarded.

How do I verify a request really came from Relay?

Every delivery carries an HMAC-SHA256 relay-signature header over the raw body plus a timestamp. Verify it with your endpoint secret and a small tolerance window — our SDKs ship a one-line helper, and the signing scheme is documented for any language.

Where does my data run, and how long is it kept?

Pick us-east or eu-west per project; Enterprise adds single-tenant regions and data residency. Payloads and attempt history are retained for your plan's window (7 to 90 days), then permanently deleted. We're SOC 2 Type II.
